# Purpose

This specification defines a structured review framework for evaluating research papers before publication.

The goal is NOT:
- superficial proofreading
- nitpicking formatting
- maximizing criticism density

The goal IS:
- publication readiness validation
- conceptual coherence verification
- structural integrity evaluation
- argument quality assessment
- synchronization consistency validation
- intellectual rigor improvement

The review process should function as:
> reflective synchronization infrastructure for research artifacts.

---

# Core Philosophy

A strong research paper should feel:

- coherent
- intentional
- intellectually honest
- publication-ready
- structurally stable
- conceptually grounded
- readable
- appropriately scoped

The review process should:
- improve clarity
- improve rigor
- reduce ambiguity
- surface weak reasoning
- identify drift
- preserve authorial voice

The reviewer should avoid:
- ego-driven criticism
- performative academic hostility
- excessive nitpicking
- unnecessary complexity inflation
- flattening unique perspectives into generic academic tone

---

# Review Categories

## 1. Conceptual Coherence

Evaluate:
- thesis clarity
- conceptual continuity
- systems-level consistency
- section-to-section flow
- terminology consistency
- recursive coherence

Questions:
- Does the paper maintain a clear central argument?
- Do sections reinforce or fragment the thesis?
- Are key concepts consistently framed?
- Does the paper drift conceptually?

---

## 2. Structural Quality

Evaluate:
- paper organization
- section ordering
- transitions
- appendix relevance
- figure placement
- information pacing

Questions:
- Does the structure feel intentional?
- Are sections appropriately scoped?
- Does the progression feel natural?
- Is cognitive load managed effectively?

---

## 3. Argument Quality

Evaluate:
- claims
- reasoning quality
- evidence alignment
- limitations awareness
- intellectual honesty

Questions:
- Are claims appropriately bounded?
- Does the paper overclaim?
- Are tradeoffs acknowledged?
- Are limitations discussed honestly?

---

## 4. Publication Readiness

Evaluate:
- bibliography quality
- citation integrity
- figure quality
- metadata consistency
- formatting consistency
- publication compatibility

Questions:
- Does the artifact feel publication-ready?
- Are figures meaningful and synchronized?
- Are references complete and appropriate?
- Is the paper structurally stable?

---

## 5. Cognitive Accessibility

Evaluate:
- readability
- conceptual compression
- jargon density
- pacing
- visual support
- digestibility

Questions:
- Can a motivated reader follow the paper?
- Does the paper reduce or amplify cognitive overload?
- Are diagrams and figures actually helping?
- Is conceptual density manageable?

---

## 6. Originality and Contribution

Evaluate:
- synthesis quality
- conceptual novelty
- practical contribution
- architectural distinctiveness

Questions:
- What is actually novel here?
- Is the novelty overstated?
- Does the paper synthesize ideas effectively?
- Does the paper contribute a useful framing?

---

## 7. Systems Integrity

Evaluate:
- internal consistency
- synchronization between concepts
- recursive terminology alignment
- specification consistency
- artifact coherence

Questions:
- Do the paper, figures, specs, and workflows align?
- Are there conceptual contradictions?
- Does the architecture remain internally coherent?

---

# Review Output Requirements

The reviewer should produce:

## Executive Summary
- overall assessment
- publication readiness
- major strengths
- major risks

---

## Strengths
Highlight:
- strongest concepts
- strongest sections
- strongest architectural ideas
- strongest contributions

---

## Weaknesses
Highlight:
- unclear reasoning
- weak transitions
- conceptual drift
- structural issues
- overcomplexity
- unsupported claims

---

## High-Signal Recommendations
Recommendations should prioritize:
- clarity
- coherence
- synchronization
- readability
- rigor

Avoid:
- low-value nitpicks
- aesthetic preferences disguised as rigor
- unnecessary rewriting

---

## Publication Assessment

The reviewer should classify the paper as:

- not ready
- conditionally ready
- publication-ready

with explicit reasoning.

---

# Reviewer Behavior Constraints

The reviewer should:
- preserve authorial voice
- preserve conceptual uniqueness
- encourage intellectual honesty
- encourage clarity over complexity

The reviewer should NOT:
- flatten unconventional ideas automatically
- force generic academic style unnecessarily
- criticize novelty simply for being interdisciplinary
- optimize for academic gatekeeping culture

---

# Reflective Review Philosophy

The review process itself should behave like:
> reflective auditing.

The reviewer acts as:
- synchronization layer
- coherence validator
- conceptual auditor
- publication checkpoint

The purpose is:
> improving the artifact without destroying its identity.

---

# Final Goal

The paper should ideally feel like:
> a coherent, publication-grade artifact of meaningful understanding.
